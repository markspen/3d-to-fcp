3D to FCP — Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 13, 2026

Short version: 3D to FCP collects no data. It runs entirely on your Mac, reads only the USDZ files you choose, and writes Motion template bundles to your local Motion Templates folder. Nothing leaves your computer.

Data we collect

None. 3D to FCP does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal information, usage data, analytics, or device identifiers. We do not use trackers, third-party SDKs, or telemetry of any kind.

How the app uses files

3D to FCP reads only the USDZ files you explicitly drag onto its window or select via the Add Files button. It writes Motion template bundles to the Motion Templates folder you grant it access to (typically ~/Movies/Motion Templates.localized/Titles.localized/). All file operations are local to your Mac. No file content, filename, or metadata is sent anywhere.

Network use

The app makes no network requests. The only outbound action is opening external links (such as the Ripple Training website) in your default browser when you click them. Those clicks open your browser and the app has no further involvement — any privacy practices on those external websites are governed by their own policies.

Permissions

On first use, 3D to FCP requests permission to read and write your Motion Templates folder. This permission is required so the app can save converted titles where Final Cut Pro can find them. Permission is stored locally as a macOS security-scoped bookmark; it is not transmitted.

Children's privacy

3D to FCP does not collect information from anyone, including children. The app is rated 4+ and contains no objectionable content.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the "Last updated" date above will be revised. Material changes will be announced on the Ripple Training website.
